FAQ - Valor Intelligence Search

What kind of data does the search engine index?
The search engine indexes publicly accessible documents that include personal information such as names, addresses, phone numbers, email addresses, and other publicly disclosed data.
Is it legal to use this search engine?
Yes, our search engine compiles publicly available data. However, users are responsible for complying with local laws regarding data privacy and usage in their jurisdiction.
How do you ensure the data is up-to-date?
We continuously update our indexes to reflect changes in the source documents. New data is added regularly, and outdated records are revised or removed as needed.
Can I request the removal of my personal information from your search engine?
Yes, if you find your personal information indexed in our search engine and wish to have it removed, you can submit a request through our removal form linked in the footer. Please note that removal from our search engine does not mean the data is deleted from the original public sources.
How do you protect the data stored in your search engine?
We employ industry-standard security measures to protect the data we index and store. However, as the data is already publicly available, our focus is on securing our systems against unauthorized access.
